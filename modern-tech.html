Greenhouses are enclosed structures used for growing plants in a controlled environment.They are often made of glass or plastic and are designed to trap sunlight, creating a warm and humid environment that is ideal for growing plants.
Hydroponics is an advanced farming technique that involves growing plants without soil. Instead, plants are grown in nutrient-rich water solutions, often using a variety of substrates like perlite, coco coir, or vermiculite to support the roots.The basic concept of hydroponics is that plants derive their nutrients from the water solution rather than soil. Nutrients are provided to the plants in precise amounts and ratios, ensuring that they receive exactly what they need to grow optimally.

    Precision Farming
Precision farming, also known as precision agriculture, is a modern farming technique that uses technology like as GPS, drones, and sensors to monitor and optimize crop and soil conditions. It enables farmers to make informed decisions about when and where to apply water, fertilizer, pesticides, and other inputs based on the specific needs of each crop and soil.
